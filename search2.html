West Godavari And it's Seasons



Not all crops grow well in every season.


Different crops have specific requirements and suitable climatic conditions.
Based on the climatic conditions, crops in India are broadly classified into two categories:


1.Kharif crops

2.Rabi crops



Points to Remember About Kharif Crops:

1.Sowing Season
Kharif crops are sown early-May – usually at the beginning of the first monsoon rains


2.Rainfall Pattern
Crops are drastically affected – too little or too much rainfall can lay waste to the efforts


3.Essential Factors
Requires hot weather and a large amount of water to grow.


4.Harvesting Months
Usually between October and November (Please note – exact harvesting season varies
according to the crops and region)


5.They are also known as Monsoon Crops


6.Example- Cotton,Groundnut,maize,rice



Points to Remember About Rabi Crops:

1.Sowing Season
Rabi crops are sown around mid November-preferably after the monsoon rains.


2.Rainfall Pattern
Generally not affected (however, rain in winter can potentially spoil rabi crops)


3.Essential Factors
Requires a warm climate for seed germination and a cold climate to grow.


4.Harvesting Months
Generally between April and May (please note – exact harvesting season varies
according to the crops and region)


5.They are also known as Winter Crops


6.Example- Barley, gram, peas and wheat
